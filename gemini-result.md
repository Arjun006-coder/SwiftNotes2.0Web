# TL;DR
Photosynthesis converts light energy, carbon dioxide, and water into sugars and oxygen, fueling life and maintaining Earth's atmosphere. Cellular respiration then breaks down biological fuels, often with oxygen, to produce ATP, the essential chemical energy currency for all cellular processes.

# Key Ideas
*   Photosynthesis is the process by which photosynthetic organisms (plants, algae, cyanobacteria) convert light energy into chemical energy (sugars) from carbon dioxide and water, releasing oxygen. It is crucial for maintaining Earth's atmospheric oxygen and supplying energy for complex life.
*   Photosynthesis occurs in two main stages: light-dependent reactions (capturing light to produce ATP and NADPH) and light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle, using ATP/NADPH to synthesize glucose from CO2).
*   Cellular respiration is the process where biological fuels are oxidized, typically with oxygen, to produce large amounts of ATP, the energy currency driving all cellular activities.
*   Respiration involves catabolic reactions that break down complex molecules into simpler ones, releasing chemical energy in a slow, controlled manner to power cellular functions like biosynthesis and transport.

# Important Details
*   **Photosynthesis Overall Equation:** 6CO2 + 6H2O + Light Energy → C6H12O6 + 6O2
*   **Chlorophyll:** Key pigments that capture light energy for photosynthesis, absorbing blue and red light while reflecting green, giving plants their characteristic color.
*   **ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate):** The primary energy currency of the cell; energy is released for cellular use when the bond of its third phosphate group is broken.
*   **Photosynthesis Inputs:** Carbon dioxide, water, and light energy.
*   **Photosynthesis Outputs:** Sugars (glucose) and oxygen.
*   **Cellular Respiration Inputs:** Biological fuels (e.g., sugar, amino acids, fatty acids) and an inorganic electron acceptor (most commonly molecular oxygen, O2).
*   **Cellular Respiration Output:** Large amounts of ATP, along with waste products.
